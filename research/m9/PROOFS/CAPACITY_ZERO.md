# M9 — Adaptive camouflage capacity is zero (k-pair, B≥2, ε<1)

**Theorem.** For the k-pair family, if the observer is adaptive with budget \(B\ge 2\) and \(\varepsilon<1\), there is **no** k such that \(D_B^{\mathrm{ad}}(P_k,Q_k)\le\varepsilon\).

**Proof.** For all k, \(D_B^{\mathrm{ad}}=1\) when \(B\ge 2\). ∎

**Contrast.** Nonadaptive risk \(D_B^{\mathrm{na}}=\min(B,k)/k\) can be driven below any \(\varepsilon\) by taking k large. Large branching helps only against checklists, not against adaptive T1 in this model.

**Product:** Do not market “more branches ⇒ safer under any T1.”
